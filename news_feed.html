Sykes award illuminates next level of sustainable energy class
2021-01-01 - UR Chem Eng News (2021)
This fall at the University of Rochester, Marc Porosoff's course CHE 150: Introduction to Sustainable Energy engaged students in a hands-on project to design and build solar hot water heaters, thanks to a $15,000 Sykes Engineering Award. The course included an in-class experiment using a photovoltaic array to demonstrate the conversion efficiencies of solar energy to electricity and then to heat, revealing a low efficiency of 9 percent. Students worked in teams to innovate beyond a commercial solar water heating kettle, experimenting with various designs to improve efficiency, with some teams achieving an average efficiency of 24% across all trials. Congratulations to the Porosoff Research Group for their innovative work in sustainable energy education!

This low-cost catalyst helps turn seawater into fuel at scale - University of Rochester
2020-07-15 - General News (Google News)
Researchers at the University of Rochester, in collaboration with the Naval Research Laboratory and other partners, have made significant progress in converting seawater into fuel by developing a low-cost, potassium-promoted molybdenum carbide catalyst that efficiently converts carbon dioxide to carbon monoxide. This advancement, described in the journal Energy & Environmental Science, represents a critical step in the process of producing fuel from seawater, a method that could potentially allow navy ships to operate continuously without the need for conventional fuel resupply. The catalyst has shown no signs of deactivation during a 10-day pilot-scale study, proving its potential for industrial scale use and further applications in capturing carbon dioxide from power plants.
