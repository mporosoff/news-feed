This low-cost catalyst helps turn seawater into fuel at scale - University of Rochester
2020-07-15 - General News (Google News)
Researchers at the University of Rochester, in collaboration with the Naval Research Laboratory, the University of Pittsburgh, and OxEon Energy, have developed a potassium-promoted molybdenum carbide catalyst that efficiently converts carbon dioxide to carbon monoxide, a crucial step in transforming seawater into fuel. This breakthrough marks the first time this type of catalyst has been demonstrated to work on an industrial scale, according to Marc Porosoff, assistant professor at the University of Rochester. The team's findings, detailed in the journal Energy & Environmental Science, involved extensive testing at molecular, laboratory, and pilot scales to confirm the catalyst's effectiveness and potential for large-scale application.

Professor Porosoff wins award to develop reverse water-gas shift catalysts
2018-01-01 - UR Chem Eng News (2018)
Prof. Porosoff has been awarded a $60,000 University Research Award to develop a novel technique for creating nano-sized reverse water-gas shift (RWGS) catalysts. This development aims to facilitate a long-term research program focused on converting CO2 into plastics, chemicals, and fuels through hydrogenation. Additionally, the project could integrate with the Advanced Materials for Powerful Lasers (AMPL) center, utilizing laser-based materials processing to enhance the synthesis of these nano-catalysts.
